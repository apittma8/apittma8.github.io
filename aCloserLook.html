  • Spring 26 - BSIT Capstone: The Sandbox Paradox (Final Paper) PDF
    This paper argues that modern sandbox games face a “Sandbox Paradox,” where AI-driven personalization and aggressive monetization quietly undermine the freedom these games promise, as hidden analytics shape player behavior, target emotional vulnerabilities, and restrict autonomy. Drawing on player reports and research, it shows how manipulative monetization, emotional targeting, data-driven profiling, and opaque AI systems can harm well-being, especially for vulnerable groups, while also raising concerns about privacy, fairness, and transparency. It concludes that developers must adopt ethical, transparent, and accountable design practices—such as open player modeling, machine unlearning, and external auditing to balance profit with player trust, autonomy, and well-being.
